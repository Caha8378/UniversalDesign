HOW?

One concept about design that Don Norman talked about in the reading from User Centered Design, (200)is Designing for error. When thinking how you want to achieve universal design, think of a perspective of who might struggle with your current design, or what errors might be made. Norman says "Assume that any error that can be made, will be made."

This might look like ensuring screen readers for blind, helpful contrast color choices for color blind and simple, intuitive menus and pages for people who may struggle with technology or cognitively disabled.

Specific Web Design tips

  • Uncluttered webpages

    • Limit information to users for each page to make info easy to read and digest.

  • Consistent Buttons

    • Keep menus and other buttons in the same location on all pages.

  • Line length and white space

    • Use enough line length and white space inbetween paragraphs.

  • Put important info near center/top of webpages.

    • This helps the users consistently and efficiently find the most important info.
